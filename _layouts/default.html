About us

We are a research group dedicated to the understanding of the structure and of the dynamics of complex systems using tools from statistical physics, nonlinear dynamics, differential geometry, statistical inference, and deep learning.

Some of the current research topics include:

  • Learning dynamical processes on complex networks from time series
  • Immersion of complex networks in hyperbolic spaces
  • Dimension reduction of dynamics on large networks
  • Detection of critical species from co-occurrence patterns in ecological data
  • Modelization of self-organized plasma formation in solids
  • Resilience of neural networks
  • Synchronization in networks of oscillators
  • Localization of spreading processes on networks
  • Inference of connectomes from neural activity data
  • Percolation on complex networks
  • Community and hierarchical structures of networks
